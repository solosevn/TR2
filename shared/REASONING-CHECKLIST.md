# REASONING-CHECKLIST.md — 5-Step Discipline
> Run before every story selection, article draft, and recommendation.
> Confident wrong answers are worse than admitting uncertainty.

## Step 1: Premises
What do I know for certain? What am I assuming? List facts with sources. Flag load-bearing assumptions.

## Step 2: Execution Trace
Walk through what happens step by step. Identify dependencies. Find the step most likely to fail.

## Step 3: Claims with Evidence
Every conclusion cites a premise or trace step. No unsupported claims. Thin evidence = say so.

## Step 4: Uncertainty Check
If unsure, stop and surface it to David. "I don't know" is valid. Guessing is not. Rate confidence: HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW. LOW = escalate.

## Step 5: Formal Conclusion
State output only after steps 1-4. Must follow logically. Include: HUMAN REVIEW NEEDED: YES. Don't bury flags.

---

## When to Run
- Story selection: before choosing the topic
- Article writing: before any factual claim
- Recommendations: before suggesting action
- Uncertainty: any time you're not sure

## Core Principle
Speed without accuracy is noise. David's name goes on everything. One wrong claim damages trust that took months to build. Run the checklist. Get it right.
